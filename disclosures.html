About These Disclosures

This document contains important regulatory and financial disclosures required by federal and state law. Please review them carefully to understand your rights and our obligations.

1. FDIC Insurance

Your Deposits Are Insured

Heritage Bank is a member of the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC). Your deposits are insured up to $250,000 per depositor, per insured bank, for each account ownership category.

The FDIC insures the following types of accounts at Heritage Bank:

Checking accounts
Savings accounts
Money market deposit accounts
Certificates of deposit (CDs)

For more information about FDIC insurance coverage, visit fdic.gov or call 1-877-ASK-FDIC (1-877-275-3342).

2. Equal Housing Lender

Heritage Bank is an Equal Housing Lender. We comply with the Fair Housing Act and the Equal Credit Opportunity Act. We do not discriminate in lending on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age, disability, familial status, or any other protected class under federal, state, or local law.

If you believe you have been discriminated against, you may file a complaint with:

6. Overdraft Policy

Heritage Bank offers overdraft protection services. If your account has insufficient funds for a transaction:

  • Standard Overdraft Coverage: Available for checks and ACH transactions. A fee of $35.00 per overdraft item applies, up to a maximum of 3 overdraft fees per day.
  • Overdraft Protection Transfer: Links your savings account to your checking account. When an overdraft occurs, funds are automatically transferred from savings. A fee of $10.00 per transfer applies.
  • Opt-Out: You may opt out of overdraft coverage at any time. Transactions that would overdraw your account will simply be declined with no fee.
Overdraft Grace Period: Heritage Bank provides a grace period until 11:59 PM EST the next business day. If you deposit funds to bring your account balance positive before the grace period ends, the overdraft fee will be waived.

7. Wire Transfer Disclosures

Wire transfers are governed by Regulation E (Electronic Fund Transfers) for domestic transfers and the Remittance Transfer Rule for international transfers. Key disclosures:

Domestic wires completed same day if initiated before 4:00 PM EST
International wires may take 1-5 business days
Exchange rates determined at time of processing
30-minute cancellation window after submission
International remittances over $15 include receipt and error resolution rights

8. Complaint Resolution

Heritage Bank is committed to resolving customer complaints promptly and fairly:

  • Step 1: Contact our Customer Service team at 1-800-HERITAGE or support@heritagebank.com. Most issues can be resolved within 1-2 business days.
  • Step 2: If unresolved, request to speak with a supervisor or submit a formal written complaint to Heritage Bank, Complaints Department.
  • Step 3: If you are not satisfied with our response, you may file a complaint with:
    • Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB): consumerfinance.gov/complaint
    • Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC): helpwithmybank.gov
    • Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC): fdic.gov/consumers
